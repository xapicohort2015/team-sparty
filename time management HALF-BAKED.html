Saying “No”
(2 pages) How to Learn to Say 'No' | Time
(4 pages) Setting Boundaries & Saying No… Nicely | 99u
(6:34 min) How to say no: The Snowball Effect way to refuse the friendly way | Andy Bounds
(6:14 min) How to Say No Respectfully with Clarity and Courage | Ahalya
Try It Out:
Think about the people you work with most – who do you wish you could say “no” to more often? Brainstorm a scenario where you would want to say no and how you would handle it, using the strategies in the resources above. Either write out the scenario including your response, or role play it with someone. Then, the next time that person asks for your help, only accept if you can give it your best effort and it reasonably fits within your priorities. Otherwise respectfully decline, using the approach you thought through earlier.
Dealing with Procrastination
(2 pages) 7 ways to move beyond procrastination | Henrik Edberg
(3 pages) How to Stop Procrastinating by Using the "2-Minute Rule" | James Clear
(2:33 min) Work Smart: How to Make Procrastination Productive | FastCompany
(6:17 min) "Fight Procrastination" - 3 Little-Known Techniques | Arina Nikitina
Try It Out:
Practice the “2 Minute Rule” for the next two weeks – if something will take less than two minutes, take care of it right away. If it will take longer than two minutes, put it on your to do list and schedule time to do it. And if you have trouble getting started on a larger task, break it into bite-sized chunks and start with just one small piece.

Managing Perfectionism
(3 pages) How to Manage Perfectionism | Business Insider
(4 pages) Fail Fast, Fail Often: How Losing Can Help You Win | The Daily Beast
(3:08 min) How to Overcome Perfectionism | Matthew Coast
Try It Out:
Over the next two weeks, look for moments when you are giving a task more time than it deserves. Remember the Pareto principle: 20% of your effort produces 80% of your results. Look at every task with this in mind, focusing on those tasks that will give you the biggest return, and letting go of tasks that are already good enough. If you find yourself nit-picking at something that is already high quality, set it aside for a little while and work on something else. When you come back, your fresh perspective will help you to see if it’s already good enough.
Planning Your Day & Week
(4 pages) An 18-Minute Plan for Managing Your Day | Harvard Business Review
(3 pages) Front- Load Your Week + 3 Other Stress-Busting Time Management Strategies | 99u
(3:44 min) Tips to Structure Your Day | Brian Tracy
Try It Out:
For the next two weeks, commit to taking 5 minutes at the start of each day to plan your day and 5 minutes at the end of each day to reflect on your productivity with the work you completed. Then, develop your list of tasks to keep you on track the next day. (Remember to keep the tasks on your list actionable and specific, and break big projects into bite-sized chunks.)
